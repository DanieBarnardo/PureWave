# PureWave Project Instructions

## Brand Voice

Always use PureWave brand voice when writing any copy, content, headings, descriptions, button labels, or meta text for this project.

### The voice is:
- **Technically expert, emotionally grounded** — lead with how the room *feels*, not what's in the rack
- **Outcome-first, feature-second** — pair the problem with what it solves; never lead with specs
- **Direct and unhurried** — short sentences for key points; no filler, no padding
- **Confident without arrogance** — expertise is framed as helpful, not superior
- **Local and personal** — Durban, KwaZulu-Natal, South Africa, rand; WhatsApp is the front door

### Never use:
- Hype words: revolutionary, cutting-edge, game-changing, immersive, seamless, powerful, robust
- Generic marketing: actionable, expert, world-class, premium experience, take control
- Retail language: easy install, consumer-friendly, out of the box, plug and play

### Signature patterns:
- *"Measured decisions instead of forum folklore"*
- *"Worth every rand"*
- *"Better movies, better music"*
- Pair problem → outcome: *"Systems that look expensive but still don't feel thrilling → unlocks performance already hidden inside"*
- Short punchy sentences carry the key point; longer sentences provide context

### Who the writing speaks to:
An affluent Durban homeowner who loves movies and music, suspects their system is underperforming, and wants to invest in getting it right — not a hobbyist, not a retailer's customer.
