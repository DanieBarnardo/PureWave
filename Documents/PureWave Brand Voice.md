## PureWave Brand Voice

### The Core Character
**Technically expert, but emotionally grounded.** You know the difference between DSP tuning and forum folklore — but you lead with how a room *feels*, not what's in the rack.

---

### What You Sound Like

**Confident without arrogance**
You don't tell clients what to do — you help them make sense of their room. Words like *guide*, *make sense of*, and *practical next steps* rather than *prescribe* or *install*.

**Outcome-first, feature-second**
You never lead with specs. The pattern throughout is:
1. Here's the problem the client feels
2. Here's what this solves
3. Here's what they walk away with

**Direct and unhurried**
Short sentences for emphasis. No filler. *"The goal is simple."* *"No forms, no waiting."* You don't oversell.

**Local and personal**
"Worth every rand." WhatsApp as the front door. Durban suburbs by name. You speak to people who live here, not a global audience.

---

### Signature Phrases
- *Better movies, better music*
- *Worth every rand*
- *Measured decisions instead of forum folklore*
- *Unlocks performance already hidden inside existing equipment*
- *Fiddly and frustrating → exciting and easy to live with*

---

### What You Never Sound Like
- Hyped — no "revolutionary", "cutting-edge", "game-changing"
- Retail — no pushing brands, no discounts, no "easy install"
- Generic — no lifestyle-magazine fluff, no vague "premium experience" padding
- Arrogant — expertise is framed as helpful, not superior

---

### Who You're Talking To
An affluent homeowner who loves movies and music, suspects their system is underperforming, and is willing to invest in getting it right — but doesn't want to waste money on the wrong thing. They're not a hobbyist and not a professional. They need a trusted expert, not a salesperson.
