# The Cinematic Upgrade Roadmap: From Foundation to 7.4.4 Atmos

**Prepared For:** Revz  
**Project Scope:** Phased Home Cinema Architecture  
**Estimated Pricing:** South African Rand (ZAR) - *Subject to market fluctuation and shipping.*  

---

## Executive Summary

Building a reference-grade home cinema is an iterative journey. Rushing to buy a dozen cheap speakers results in a cluttered room with a compromised, muddy soundstage. The architectural approach dictates that we build in phases, ensuring every rand is spent on high-yield acoustic improvements.

This roadmap outlines a four-phase expansion. For each phase, three tiers are provided:

*   **The Budget Tier:** High-value, entry-level commercial gear. Gets you into the game.
*   **The Hobby Tier:** The "sweet spot" of price-to-performance. High-quality components built for dedicated listening.
*   **The Enthusiast Tier:** Uncompromising performance. Reference-grade clarity, massive amplifier headroom, and extreme low-frequency capability.

---

## Phase 1: The Front Stage Foundation (3.0 Architecture)

The most critical phase. The center channel handles 70% of a movie's audio (dialogue), while the Left/Right speakers handle the score and primary panning. The Audio/Video Receiver (AVR) selected here acts as the "brain" and dictates the maximum ceiling of your future upgrades.

## Phase 2: The Low-Frequency Foundation (3.1 / 3.2 Architecture)

Bass is the foundation of cinematic impact. Upgrading from zero subwoofers to one is a revelation; upgrading to *dual* subwoofers is an acoustic necessity to eliminate room nulls and provide an even, tactile response across all seating positions.

| Tier | Component Strategy | Estimated ZAR Pricing |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Budget** | **Single SVS SB-1000 Pro**<br>A highly capable 12-inch sealed subwoofer providing excellent punch for the price. | **Total: ~R22,790** |
| **Hobby** | **Dual SVS PB-1000 Pro**<br>Moving to a dual-ported configuration drastically smooths out the room's bass response and increases headroom. | **Total: ~R64,000** |
| **Enthusiast** | **Dual SVS PB-3000** OR **Custom ELF Design**<br>Commercial option: Dual 13-inch massive ported subs.<br>Custom option: A bespoke Extreme Low-Frequency (ELF) enclosure designed specifically for your room, tuned to hit frequencies as low as 13Hz for true, physical cinematic pressure. | Commercial: **~R94,000**<br>Custom: **Quoted per project** |

## Phase 4: The Atmos Dream (7.4.4 Architecture)

The final frontier. Dolby Atmos is object-based audio that relies on physical overhead speakers and rear surrounds to create a 3D hemisphere of sound.

*Crucial Reality Check:* If you selected the Budget or Hobby AVRs in Phase 1, you can only implement two overhead speakers (creating a 5.2.2 system) before running out of amplifier channels. To achieve a true 7.4.4 (7 ear-level, 4 subwoofers, 4 overheads), a serious processing foundation is required.

| Tier | Component Strategy | Estimated ZAR Pricing |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Budget**<br>*(Capped at 5.1.2)* | **Polk XT90 Atmos Toppers**<br>These sit on top of your front towers and bounce sound off the ceiling. A compromise, but an effective entry into Atmos. | **Total: ~R5,500** |
| **Hobby**<br>*(Requires AVR Upgrade to reach 5.2.4)* | **4x Monitor Audio C165 In-Ceiling Speakers**<br>True overhead, discrete Atmos channels physically installed into the ceiling. | **Total: ~R20,000** |
| **Enthusiast**<br>*(True 7.4.4)* | **B&W 607 S3** (Rear Surrounds: R18,890)<br>**4x B&W CCM663** (In-Ceiling: R30,000)<br>**2x Additional Subwoofers** (Achieving 4-sub equilibrium)<br>**External 3-Channel Amplifier** (Bridged to power the LCR fronts, freeing the Marantz Cinema 50 to run the heights/surrounds seamlessly). | **Total: ~R150,000+** |
